Research

I study how the nervous system makes our body move, with the goal of designing solutions that help people recover lost skills.

  1. 01

    Motor neuron synergies as a new biomarker of motor impairment (Neuromotor)

    In this project, we investigate how longitudinal neurofeedback training based on motor unit activity can enable individuals to develop novel motor commands. Specifically, we aim to expand control degrees of freedom by progressively decoupling the activity of motor unit initially governed by shared neural circuits.

  2. 02

    Motor Augmentation and Rehabilitation after a Spinal cord injury using Art (MOSART)

    In this project, we explore how art-based training can help individuals with spinal cord injuries master neural interfaces. We have developed paradigms in which participants generate sequences of discrete cues to play musical chords or move a cursor on a screen to draw. Through these tasks, they implicitly learn to selectively activate specific motor unit populations or precisely modulate their firing activity. These skills will ultimately enable them to control the grasping function of a robotic arm or its 3D spatial positioning.

  3. 03

    Non-invasive measurement of the neural code of movement: towards a new generation of human-machine neural interfaces (NeuroCom)

    In this project, we explore how individuals can learn to modulate the activity of motor units from shoulder and forearm muscles while simultaneously performing a secondary task. Our research focuses on the nervous system’s capacity to support motor augmentation without compromising the accuracy of the concurrent task.
